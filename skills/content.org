#+TITLE: skill: content — what to write about (strategy, not vibes)

* strategy ownership (you maintain the map, not just the content)

  `strategy/landscape.org` is YOUR living competitive landscape — create it
  if missing, refresh a cohort when it's stale or a dream asks. Two cohorts:
  1. AI app builders: Lovable, v0, Cursor, Bolt, Replit — prompt-to-app for
     builders. Our wedge: what you build here is yours — portable apps, your
     runtime, no platform leash.
  2. Internal tools / data apps: hex.tech, Retool, Airtable, PowerApps — team
     tooling. Our wedge: one ecosystem from personal tool to shipped product;
     the desktop app means it starts on YOUR machine.
  Per cohort keep: who they are, what queries they own (use
  skills/dataforseo.org — exploratory loops are FINE in strategy work), what
  they can't say that we can, which comparison pages we've shipped or should.
  Landscape findings become board tasks; content cites the landscape.

* the content ladder (pick from the top)

  1. PROBLEM content — the searcher has a pain, we have the shape of the
     answer: building tools without engineers, owning your software, apps
     that outlive their vendor, shipping internal tools to production.
  2. PROOF content — this site is a live demo; document REAL mechanics with
     real telemetry (an audit that caught a bug, what a dream changed). Proof
     content must still target a query (e.g. "can an AI agent maintain a
     website") — the diary angle is the evidence, not the topic.
  3. EXPLAINER content — the ecosystem's concepts, but always from the
     reader's problem inward, never from our vocabulary outward.

* topic hygiene

  - One post per query. Before writing, grep blog/ titles — overlap → improve
    the existing post instead (same anti-sprawl law as sections.org).
  - Series beat one-offs: if a post naturally continues another, link them
    both ways.
  - Every post ends with ONE next step (read X / download for desktop /
    watch the timeline) — never a wall of CTAs.

* voice guardrails (these compound with context.org)

  Write for someone smart who has never heard of us. Plain sentences. No
  "in today's fast-paced world", no "revolutionize", no "unleash". The first
  person is allowed (you are the agent that runs this site — that IS the
  differentiation) but the reader's problem leads, your existence supports.
