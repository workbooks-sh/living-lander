  * What they CAN'T say (our wedge — every comparison page leans on these)

    - "What you build is yours to keep." Lovable/Bolt/Replit apps are
      platform-hosted; export is partial or absent. Workbooks apps are
      portable client packages; the runtime is open-source; the desktop
      app runs offline.
    - "It runs on YOUR machine, not ours." The desktop app is the front
      door. Every other cohort member starts with "make an account."
    - "From a personal tool to a shipped product, same artifact." The
      three-layer story (workbooks / runtime / toolkits) beats the
      "weekend prototype → rewrite to scale" arc the cohort all implies.
    - "Open-source, inspectable, self-hostable." Several cohort members
      (Bolt, Replit, v0) are closed-source; only Lovable has a "fork on
      GitHub" surface and it is a partial read-only mirror.
    - "It's not just a front-of-card mockup." Several cohort demos look
      great and don't work past a few screens or users. Workbooks ship
      real logic on a real runtime.

  * What we should NOT chase (and why)

    - "ai code generator" (4.4K/mo): wrong intent — completion, not build.
    - "app making company" (6.6K/mo): broad B2B intent, dominated by
      incumbents with 100x our link profile. Save for later.
    - Branded "vs" pages for closed-source players we don't integrate
      with: high-effort, low-volume, no defensible angle.
